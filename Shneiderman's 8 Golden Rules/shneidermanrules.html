Shneiderman's 8 Golden Rules

After reading Shneiderman's 8 golden rules, I feel like the rule that is most absent in my own microproject is #2, universal usability. Right now, my project does not facilitate the transformation of content/interaction very much. Both novice and expert users have access to the same exact features and functionality. There are no shortcuts and minimal explanations.
The rule that would improve my sketchbook interface the most at this point in time would probably be #3, offering informative feedback. There could be more visual hierarchy as well as general feedback for actions such as when a user hovers over or clicks a link.
I think the hardest rule to implement honestly is #7, keeping users in control, because when someone is designing an interface, they may not be taking into account how a user feels about the interface or what they want out of it. The designer's vision and preferences may not always align with the users'. That's why user research and testing is so important, to make sure that the users are in control and that the interface is designed with their needs in mind.
Finally, I think one rule that kind of contradicted how I designed my microproject orginally is #6, the easy reversal of actions. In my project, the main goal for the user was to finish the task/game in an alloted amount of time, and once they started the game there was no way of going back or pausing. This may create some frustration in the user, but at the same time, frustration was one of the feelings I wanted to evoke while interacting with this project.
